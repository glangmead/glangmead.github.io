2 Introduction

Following David Jaz Myers in (Myers, 2022), we define a cover as follows:

Definition 1.1. A map \(\pi:E\to B\) is a cover if it is \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\)-étale and its fibers are sets.

Recall that \(\pi\) being \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\)-étale means that the naturality square \[\begin{CD} E @>(-)^{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}>> \text{\textesh}{}_{1}E \\ @V{\pi}VV @VV{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\pi}V \\ B @>>(-)^{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}> \text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \end{CD}\] is a pullback, which means among other things that each corresponding fiber of the vertical maps is equivalent.

As David proves in (Myers, 2022), the type of \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\)-étale maps into \(B\) is equivalent to the function type \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}\). Since a cover has the further condition that the fibers are sets, this means:

Lemma 1.1. The type of covers over \(B\) is equivalent to \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{0}}\).

In homotopy type theory we pass easily between the vertical picture (covers of \(B\)) and the horizontal picture (classifying maps into some type of types). But when we unpack this a little bit we find important classical stories. If we equip \(B\) with a basepoint \(*_B\) then a map \(f:\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{0}}\) is an action of the fundamental groupoid \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B\) on the set \(f(*_B)\).
