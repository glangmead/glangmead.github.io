2 Introduction

Following David Jaz Myers in (Myers, 2022), we define a cover as follows:

Definition 1.1. A map \(\pi:E\to B\) is a cover if it is \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\)-étale and its fibers are sets.

Recall that \(\pi\) being \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\)-étale means that the naturality square \[\begin{CD} E @>(-)^{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}>> \text{\textesh}{}_{1}E \\ @V{\pi}VV @VV{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\pi}V \\ B @>>(-)^{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}> \text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \end{CD}\] is a pullback, which means among other things that each corresponding fiber of the vertical maps is equivalent.

As David proves in (Myers, 2022), the type of \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}\)-étale maps into \(B\) is equivalent to the function type \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}\). Since a cover has the further condition that the fibers are sets, this means:

Lemma 1.1. The type of covers over \(B\) is equivalent to \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{0}}\).

In homotopy type theory we pass easily between the vertical picture (covers of \(B\)) and the horizontal picture (classifying maps into some type of types). But when we unpack this a little bit we find important classical stories. If we equip \(B\) with a basepoint \(*_B\) then a map \(f:\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{0}}\) is an action of the fundamental groupoid \(\text{\textesh}{}_{1}B\) on the set \(f(*_B)\), which is the fiber over \(*_B\).

Next, consider the type of 2-covers:

Definition 1.2. A map \(\pi:E\to B\) is a 2-cover if it is \(\text{\textesh}{}_{2}\)-étale and its fibers are groupoids.

Lemma 1.2. The type of 2-covers over \(B\) is equivalent to \(\text{\textesh}{}_{2}B \to \mathsf{Type}_{\text{\textesh}{}_{1}}\).

Consider the following classical story:

Covering spaces are like principal bundles where the group is topologically discrete. Loops in the base space lift to paths in the covering space, but contractible loops lift to contractible loops. And in general, elements of \(\pi_1(B)\) lift to paths in the covering space that connect different elements of the fiber. But if the group \(G\) is a Lie group, then even contractible paths in \(B\) can lift to nontrivial automorphisms of \(G\).
